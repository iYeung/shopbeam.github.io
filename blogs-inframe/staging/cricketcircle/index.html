Home Products Layette Bodysuit (Longsleeve)

Layette

Once upon a time – a sad, sad time before infant Air Jordans, Mini Rodini and baby jeggings – a family member would gift you "the layette", that is, every single thing in your baby’s first wardrobe, from booties to blankets. We’re not fans of wearing one brand head-to-toe (although if forced, we’d make do with The Row), so here we’re recommending our favorite pieces a la carte. Like in your own wardrobe, there are no wrong answers, so have fun. Few things will pick you up in an exhausted moment like a too-cute-for-words playsuit.

  1. Bodysuit (Shortsleeve)

    • A white bodysuit (aka "Onesie") is the little black dress of baby wear
    • Defined by a snap-crotch, these getups help keep diapers in place
    • Your baby will likely wear one everyday, all day, for the first year or so
    • Fun fact: The "lap shoulder" neckline, aka the overlapping panels at the shoulder, allows you to remove a soiled bodysuit down over the shoulder and hips instead of over the head!
    • Buy the multi-packs, you’ll need them
    Brands we love
    Burberry (as pictured),
    Florence Eiseman,
    Stella McCartney

  2. Bodysuit (Longsleeve)

    • The cooler-weather version of the baby essential known as a Onesie
    • It’s common to see little ones run around in nothing but this
    • White ones will get you more mileage, but "fashion" colors/patterns can be paired with leggings or pants, too
    • We prefer the short-sleeve version as it works better for layering
    Brands we love
    Stella McCartney (as pictured),
    Gucci,
    Burberry

  3. Kimono-Style Tops

    • Designed to make dressing newborns easy, as they hate having clothing pulled over their weak and wobbly head
    • These are also helpful for a newborn before her umbilical cord falls off (1-2 weeks), since it won’t rub like a bodysuit might
    • Buy anything thin, soft and in solid colors
    Brands we love
    Offspring (as pictured),
    Little Marc Jacobs,
    More Offspring

  4. Playsuits

    • AKA one-pieces, sleep-and-play suits, footies, footed sleepers, coveralls â itâs an all-in-one outfit
    • Separates ride up on plump baby bodies; also, why not avoid a binding waistband while you can
    • Footed styles allow you to skip pointless socks (they always slip off)
    • Try 6 with feet, 6 without feet
    • Non-snap closures are easiest for the first few months of nonstop diaper changing (try magnet or zipper styles)
    • Mitt pocket (i.e. fold-back mittens on the sleeves) prevent your baby from scratching his face
    Brands we love
    Giggle Better Basics (as pictured),
    Zip It,
    Magnificent Baby

  5. Gowns

    • A blessing for middle-of-night diaper changes — no snaps or zippers — just hike it up
    • Don’t hesitate to put your boy in a gown — it’s pretty common (and super cute)
    • Mitt pocket (i.e. fold-back mittens on the sleeves) keep sharp fingernails from inflicting nocturnal scratches
    Brands we love
    KicKee Pants (as pictured),
    Kissy Kissy,
    Little Giraffe

  6. Pants

    • You could skip these all together, we think it looks funny to see a baby in pants
    • Should be soft and comfy, no zippers, no buttons, no tight waistbands
    • Avoid in first two weeks so the waistband doesn’t irritate the umbilical cord
    Brands we love
    Mabo (as pictured),
    Paige Lauren,
    Splendid

  7. Shirts

    • There’s not much of a point — we say skip it
    • Bodysuits are much easier to dress your newborn in and look cuter overall
    • If you’re dying to purchase "separates", find tops with a "lap shoulder" neckline, those cute overlapping shoulder panels, which allows you to open the neckline wide to fit that big baby head.
    Brands we love
    Splendid (as pictured),
    Paige Lauren,
    Petit Bateau

  8. Sweaters

    • Not essential in the early going since the baby’s often swaddled in a blanket and held close
    • Zippered or button-up styles are the easiest to put on and off
    • A hood is a great option to have
    • The softer the fabric, the better
    Brands we love
    J. Crew (as pictured),
    Little Giraffe,
    KicKee Pants

  9. Dresses

    • Plan to dress her with a pair of leggings or bloomers underneath depending on the season
    • Impractical for the crawling or toddling stages
    • Cute for special occasions and for confident walkers
    Brands we love
    Ralph Lauren (as pictured),
    Mabo,
    Splendid

  10. Infant Caps

    • Cotton skullcaps are a handy layer for the cooler months since you can usually add or remove it without waking the baby
    • You’ll likely receive a zillion hats as gifts so buy sparingly
    Brands we love
    Nature Baby for J. Crew (as pictured),
    babyGap,
    Giggle Better Basics

  11. Seasonal Hats

    • Babies’ head-to-body ratio makes hats important — they lose heat rapidly through the head; but that’s easily fixed
    • Velcro, ties or snaps are essential; as soon as the baby can pull it off, she will
    • Thick cotton or sun protecting synthetic for summer hats; wool, fleece or double-lined cotton in winter
    Brands we love
    7 A.M. Enfant (as pictured),
    iPlay,
    Hanna Andersson

  12. Booties

    • Purely for looks; socks or footy outfits are more practical for warmth
    • There’s nothing sadder (or more common) than losing a bootie on the first wear — pick styles with drawstring ties or adjustable snap closures
    Brands we love
    Nature Baby for J. Crew (as pictured),
    Zutano,
    Ralph Lauren

  13. Socks

    • Athletic-style socks with thick elastic at the top stay on better than others (but all slip off easily)
    • They can double as mittens to prevent your baby from scratching her face
    • Grippies on the bottoms are to prevent walkers from slipping — but that’s still a year off
    Brands we love
    Etiquette (as pictured),
    Gerber,
    Trumpette

  14. Swaddle

    • Two schools of thought on this: the instant-Velcro (or zipper) swaddle, or the traditional thin cotton swaddle, which requires technique
    • We recommend the instant method (you’ll find the latter listed under Receiving Blankets)
    • The Velcro and zipper styles are a quick and foolproof way to bundle your babe as snug as a taco
    • Depending on the season, choose cotton or a warmer fleecy fabric
    • Try a few to see which you prefer but any will be better than going rogue and trying to do it yourself
    Brands we love
    Halo (as pictured),
    The Woombie,
    Summer Infant SwaddleMe

  15. Sleep Sack

    • It’s basically a sleeveless, wearable blanket
    • Use it after the swaddling phase (around 4 months onward) to keep the baby warm at night
    • Allows the coziness of a blanket, without the hazard of loose fabric near the face
    • Best when worn atop a long-sleeve bodysuit
    Brands we love
    Baby Deedee (as pictured),
    Carter’s,
    Aden + Anais

  16. Bunting

    • This snowsuit-type getup has limited use since it’s unsafe to wear bulky layers while secured in a car seat
    • Works for winter babies being transported via front-pack, or for snowy photo ops
    • Zip-up styles are easiest to get on and off
    Brands we love
    babyGap (as pictured),
    iPlay,
    7 A.M. Enfant

  17. Receiving Blankets

    • A soft, thin blanket that travels with the baby when he or she is passed around
    • Somewhere soft for baby to rest his head; handy for blotting spit-up
    • Everyone's going to buy you a blankie — so start with just a couple to tide you over until all the gifts roll in
    • Can also be used as a tummy time blanket
    • We’re including traditional muslin swaddles in this category since they’re so handy
    • In the early months, stacks of muslin swaddles are not always enough (but they’re obsolete quickly!)
    • Perfect for putting over the hood of the stroller to block the sun or keep out the germs when you’re on the go
    Brands we love
    J. Crew (as pictured),
    Little Giraffe,
    Aden + Anais

  18. Take-Home Outfit

    • The first "look" for baby’s post-hospital debut
    • Paparazzi will likely not be waiting, so it’s really just for you
    • Keep in mind, they’ll wear it again — it’s basically just your favorite outfit they have so far
    • Newborns are always smaller than you expect so a pants and top get-up is probably overkill
    • In the cooler months, we suggest including a hooded zip up sweater or warmer layer
    Brands we love
    Jacadi (as pictured),
    Tane Organics,
    Bonpoint

  19. Towels

    • Babies grow quickly, so start with 6-12 medium-size towels
    • Just because it’s soft doesn’t mean it’ll absorb; stick with all-cotton
    • The hooded kind are adorable and helpful; you’ll want to warm that peach fuzz quickly
    Brands we love
    3 Sprouts (as pictured),
    Restoration Hardware,
    Land of Nod

  20. Washcloths

    • Baby washcloths are small and thin so that you can wipe clean tiny nooks and crannies like ears and between toes
    • You’ll go through at least two per bath, so make sure to have plenty on hand
    • The glove version can make baby-handling even easier
    Brands we love
    Aden + Anais (as pictured),
    Kushies,
    iPlay
